# Chapter 0: 5 Minutes — Watch an AI App Come to Life

> Lynn works in operations at TechStore — a mid-sized electronics e-commerce company that sells phones, computers, accessories, you name it.
>
> One day after lunch, a colleague casually mentioned: "Have you heard of Dify? Apparently you can build AI apps without writing any code."
>
> Lynn was skeptical: "I don't even know Python."
>
> "Seriously, no coding. You just drag and drop."
>
> At 3 PM, Lynn opened the Dify website with a "why not" attitude.

## Create an Account

1. Open your browser and go to [cloud.dify.ai](https://cloud.dify.ai)
2. Click the "Get Started" button in the top right
3. You can sign in with a GitHub account, Google account, or register with email
4. After registration, you'll land on the Dify workspace

The whole process takes less than a minute.

## Explore Existing Apps

Don't rush into building just yet. First, see what others have made.

1. Click "Explore" in the left sidebar
2. You'll see an app marketplace filled with all kinds of AI app templates
3. Find a simple one like "Translation Assistant" or "Article Summary"
4. Click "Run," enter some text, and see what happens

You'll see the AI process your input according to preset rules and return a result.

Notice — this isn't a casual chat. It's an AI application with a specific function, which is a very different thing from chatting directly with ChatGPT.

## See How It's Built

Now let's peek under the hood.

1. On the app detail page, click "Add to Workspace"
2. Go back to "Studio" — you'll see the app in your list
3. Click on it to enter the editing interface
4. You'll see a visual interface showing the app's "recipe" — which model it uses, what prompts are set, and how input/output are defined

This is what Dify does: it lets you assemble AI capabilities into an application using a visual interface.

## Look Back at What You Just Did

That was five minutes. You registered an account, ran a pre-built AI app, and opened it up to see how it was put together.

::: tip Key Insight
AI apps aren't black magic. A model, a prompt, inputs, outputs — that's basically it. What Dify does is lay all of these pieces out in the open so you can modify them, tweak them, or build from scratch.
:::
